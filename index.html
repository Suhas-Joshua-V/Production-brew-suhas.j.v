Introduction

The Workshop Experience

Production Brew is a hands-on, real-world workshop that takes students through the fundamentals of web development and modern software deployment. Over five days, participants are introduced to everything from basic IT concepts all the way to deploying live websites using professional-grade tools.

This website documents my personal journey through the workshop — what I learned each day, my honest reflections, and an overall review of the experience.

🖥️

IT Fundamentals

Day 1 covered the core concepts: CPU, RAM, GPU, storage, client-server architecture, and how the internet works at its core.

🌐

Web & Networking

Day 2 was all about websites, hosting, DNS, domains, SSH, terminal commands, and WordPress — a real-world setup day.

🚀

Git & Deployment

Day 3 introduced the full dev pipeline — Git, GitHub, Vercel, and how to push a live site from scratch using Claude as an AI assistant.

About

What is Production Brew?

Production Brew is a student-focused tech workshop designed to bridge the gap between classroom theory and industry-level practice. Unlike traditional courses, it emphasises learning by doing — students work with real tools used by professional developers every single day.

The workshop is structured across five days, each building on the last. Instructors guide students through setup, configuration, and actual deployment — ensuring that by the end, participants have not only learned concepts but have produced something real and live on the internet.

"The goal isn't just to teach code — it's to teach the mindset of a developer: build, break, fix, and ship."

Final Review

My Honest Take

💬

Student Perspective

Honestly, Production Brew was one of the more practical learning experiences I've had. The first couple of days were a bit heavy on theory — going through IT terms, networking concepts, and how the internet works — but it all started to make sense once we got our hands on actual tools.

What I genuinely appreciated was how the workshop didn't baby-step everything. We were thrown into real terminals, actual servers, and live deployment pipelines. Yes, there were moments where I had no clue what was going on, but that's where the instructors stepped in to explain things clearly.

The introduction to AI tools like Claude was a pleasant surprise. Learning how to use prompts effectively — structuring them with WHO, WHAT, HOW, and CONTENT — was a skill I didn't expect to walk away with, but it's genuinely useful.

If I had to be critical: some days felt a bit rushed, and I would have appreciated more time on certain topics. But overall, it's a solid workshop for students who want to get serious about web development and modern deployment workflows.
